Volunteer pathway

Join hands with us. Become part of a volunteer task force of document accessibility specialists — making education accessible, one document at a time.

Every volunteer starts at Tier 1. Progress is based on documents completed and quality scores from reviewers.

Tier 1

Volunteer Learner

No experience needed

You start by learning the foundations — writing image descriptions, understanding reading order, and marking decorative elements. Every task you complete makes a real document accessible for a student who otherwise could not read it.

  • Mentorship from experienced Guild Members
  • Free structured training
  • Letter of participation after your first 5 documents
Tier 2

Volunteer Contributor

10+ documents completed

You handle full document remediation — tables, forms, reading order, and basic STEM content. Your work directly supports universities in the Global South that cannot afford commercial remediation services.

  • EquitableDocs Associate certificate
  • Access to more complex and impactful documents
  • Priority assignment on urgent student requests
Tier 3

Volunteer Reviewer

30+ documents, 80%+ quality score

You take on the hardest documents — STEM textbooks, complex layouts, multi-language content. You review other members' work before it reaches students. Your quality decisions set the standard for the entire Guild.

  • Named publicly on the platform
  • Professional reference letter
  • Pathway to recognised accessibility certifications
Tier 4

Volunteer Trainer

Volunteer Reviewer + trainer pathway completed

You train university staff and new Guild Members — helping institutions build the capacity to produce accessible documents themselves. This is the 'capacity, not dependency' model in action.

  • Deliver training at partner universities
  • EquitableDocs Certified Trainer credential
  • Recognised expertise in document accessibility
Tier 5

Volunteer Mentor

Invitation only

You shape the Guild's standards, mentor members, and represent the initiative externally. You help decide how EquitableDocs grows and which institutions we support next.

  • Advisory role in platform decisions
  • Represent the Guild at conferences and with partners
  • Mentor the next generation of accessibility specialists

Common questions

Frequently asked questions

Do I need any experience to join?+
No. Tier 1 (Accessibility Apprentice) is designed for people with no prior remediation experience. You receive free training, mentorship, and guided tasks. All you need is a computer, a web browser, and the willingness to learn.
How much time do I need to commit?+
There is no minimum time commitment. Tasks have a 4-hour lock window — if you accept a task, you have 4 hours to complete it. After that, it goes back to the queue for someone else. You can work as much or as little as your schedule allows.
Is this paid work?+
The Document Accessibility Guild is volunteer-driven. You contribute your skills and time to help students with disabilities access their education. In return, you receive structured training, recognised credentials, professional reference letters, and a clear pathway from apprentice to trainer. The value at every tier is genuine professional growth in a field where skilled practitioners are urgently needed.
What credentials will I earn?+
After completing 10 documents with a passing quality score, you earn the EquitableDocs Associate Certificate. Senior Accessibility Specialists (Tier 3+) receive professional reference letters and are named on the platform. Tier 4 members earn the EquitableDocs Certified Trainer credential. All tiers receive a letter of participation documenting their contribution.
What software do I need?+
None. The Guild portal is entirely browser-based. You see your assigned pages or elements in the browser, submit your work in the browser, and track your progress in the browser. It works on any device — desktop, laptop, tablet, or phone.
Can I join from outside India?+
Yes. The Guild is open to anyone, anywhere. Language Specialists are particularly valuable if you are fluent in languages that our OCR and remediation pipeline supports. All work is remote and browser-based.
