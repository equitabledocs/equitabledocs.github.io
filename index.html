A Document Equity Initiative

Every student deserves to read their own textbooks.

In India, over 93% of academic PDFs fail basic accessibility checks. Students who are blind, have low vision, dyslexia, or other print disabilities cannot access the documents their education depends on. That's what we're here to change.

The accessibility gap in Indian higher education

Document accessibility in India is broken. The tools exist. The expertise is scarce. And the people doing the work are not valued.

Rs 80–150/page

What commercial remediators charge universities for document accessibility. Most institutions cannot afford this at the scale their students need.

Rs 12/page

What the person actually doing the remediation work earns. The rest goes to intermediaries. The people with the skills are the least compensated.

Zero career pathway

No structured training. No recognised credentials. No professional identity. Document remediation in India is informal work with no progression.

We train your staff, then step back.

EquitableDocs builds accessible document capacity inside your institution. We assess your documents, set up the right tools, train your staff to run them, and hand over. Within 12 to 18 months, the goal is for your university to process accessible documents independently.

Universities pay only the actual cost of processing. No profit margin. No hidden fees. Every cost is published on our transparency page.

Document pipeline setup

We install and configure the tools inside your infrastructure. Your documents stay on your servers.

Staff training

Structured programmes covering document creation, PDF remediation, and quality assurance — free of cost.

STEM content support

Support for STEM content including equations, complex tables, multi-column layouts, and MathML output.

Format templates

Custom document templates that teach the tagging engine how to process your institution's specific document types.

Need a document made accessible? We do that, for free.

If you have a document you cannot read because of a disability, send it to us. We will make it work with your screen reader, magnifier, or other assistive tool. There is no cost, no conditions, and no complicated process.

1

Send us your document

Create a free account on our portal and upload your document. You can also email it to deepa@equitabledocs.org — no account needed.

We accept PDF, Word, and PowerPoint files. Scanned documents are fine too.

2

We make it accessible

Our tools and a trained reviewer check and fix the document so it works with screen readers and other assistive tools.

Standard: 5–7 business days
STEM: 10 business days
3

We send it back to you

We email you when your document is ready. Download it from your account, or we send it back the way you sent it.

The Accessibility Collective

Document remediation in India is informal work. No training, no credentials, no career path. The people doing this work deserve better.

The Accessibility Collective is a community of practice hosted by EquitableDocs. Members contribute their skills to making academic documents accessible for students — and build genuine professional expertise in the process.

Free training

Structured programmes in document accessibility, from fundamentals to STEM specialist skills.

Recognised credentials

Certificates and professional references based on demonstrated, peer-reviewed work.

Real documents

Work on actual student requests — building a portfolio of meaningful, verified contributions.

Flexible commitment

Contribute as much or as little as you can. No minimum hours. Remote and asynchronous.
