About EquitableDocs

Nothing about us without us.

EquitableDocs exists because millions of students with disabilities cannot access the documents their education depends on. We are building the infrastructure to change that — openly, transparently, and at no cost to the students who need it.

The story

Why Document Equity matters

In Indian higher education, over 93% of PDFs fail basic accessibility checks. This means a blind student using a screen reader hears silence. A student with dyslexia cannot use text-to-speech. A student with a motor disability cannot navigate the document with a keyboard.

The problem is not technology. Tools exist to make documents accessible. The problem is that these tools are expensive, the expertise is scarce, and institutions do not have the infrastructure to use them at scale.

EquitableDocs addresses this by building two things: a free, automated pipeline that students can use directly, and a capacity-building programme that helps universities become self-sufficient in accessible document production within 12 to 18 months.

We are not a commercial remediation company. We do not charge students. We do not take a profit margin on university partnerships. Every cost is published openly on our transparency page. Our model is: capacity, not dependency.

The Accessibility Collective is a community of practice of skilled practitioners who contribute their expertise to making documents accessible for students across the Global South. Members receive structured training, recognised credentials, and professional reference letters — building genuine expertise in a field where skilled practitioners are urgently needed.

Our values

What guides every decision we make

These are not aspirations. They are constraints. Every feature, every page, every partnership is tested against them.

Capacity, not dependency

We train university staff to run the pipeline independently. Our success is measured by how quickly we become unnecessary.

Nothing about us without us

The disability community leads design decisions. Every feature is tested with the people who will use it. Accessibility is not an afterthought.

Radical transparency

Every cost is published. Every process is documented. Universities see exactly what their money pays for. There is no hidden margin.

Professional standing for practitioners

Collective members are skilled practitioners who contribute their expertise. Their work earns recognised credentials, professional reference letters, and genuine expertise in a growing field.

Free for students. Always.

Students with disabilities never pay. University partnerships fund the infrastructure. This is non-negotiable.

Accessibility is the product

The EquitableDocs website itself meets WCAG 2.1 AA and W3C COGA guidelines. If our own platform is not accessible, we have no right to offer accessibility services.

The founder

Deepa Palaniappan

Deepa Palaniappan founded EquitableDocs after years of working in accessibility and seeing the same pattern repeat: institutions wanted to do the right thing but lacked the tools, the training, and the infrastructure to make it happen at scale.

EquitableDocs is her answer to that gap: an open, transparent, mission-driven initiative that builds capacity where it is needed most — inside the institutions themselves — and gives document accessibility practitioners the professional recognition their work deserves.
