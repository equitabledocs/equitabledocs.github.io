Privacy policy

This policy explains what information we collect, why we collect it, and how we protect it. We have written it in plain language because we believe privacy policies should be understandable by everyone.

Who we are

EquitableDocs is a programme of The Document Equity Initiative. We make academic documents accessible for students with disabilities. Our website is equitabledocs.org.

What we collect

When you upload a document:

  • Your name and email address (so we can send you the accessible version)
  • The document you upload (so we can make it accessible)
  • Your preferred language and output format

When you register for the Document Accessibility Guild:

  • Your name, email, and location
  • Your experience level and areas of interest
  • Languages you speak

When a university enquires about partnership:

  • Institution name, country, and contact details
  • Document types, volume estimates, and service interests

Why we collect it

Only to provide the service you requested. We do not sell, share, or use your information for advertising. Ever.

Where your data is stored

  • Form submissions are processed through Formspree (formspree.io). Formspree's privacy policy applies to form data in transit.
  • Documents submitted for processing are stored on EquitableDocs servers only.
  • We do not store documents on third-party cloud services except for specific processing steps (e.g. Mathpix for equation extraction in STEM documents).

How long we keep it

  • Student documents: deleted 90 days after delivery
  • Institutional documents: deleted 180 days after delivery, or per the agreed retention period in the letter of understanding
  • Guild registration data: kept for as long as you are an active member. You can request deletion at any time.

Your rights

You can ask us to:

  • Show you what data we hold about you
  • Correct any inaccurate data
  • Delete your data entirely
  • Send you a copy of your data

Email deepa@equitabledocs.org with any of these requests. We will respond within 7 working days.

Cookies

This website does not use cookies. We do not track your browsing behaviour.

Changes to this policy

If we change this policy, we will update this page with the new date. We will not reduce your rights without telling you.

Last updated: 23 March 2026.
